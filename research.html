Research

My research applies microeconometric techniques to historical datasets, focusing on the evolution of mutualism and cooperative production in the 19th century.

Working Papers

Economic History Mutualism

"Mutual Insurance and the Industrial Revolution: Evidence from Friendly Society Returns"

2024

This paper uses newly digitised returns from 1855-1880 to examine how mutual insurance networks adapted to the shocks of late-stage industrialisation in Britain.

Political Economy Cooperatives

"The Political Economy of Co-operative Production in Victorian Ireland"

With: Eoin McLaughlin

2023

We investigate the factors predicting the success of early co-operative creameries, highlighting the role of local political stability and existing credit networks.

Data Science Methodology

"Digitising the Past: A New Dataset of British Friendly Society Financial Records"

2023

A methodological overview of the OCR and data cleaning pipeline developed to extract structured longitudinal data from Parliamentary returns.
